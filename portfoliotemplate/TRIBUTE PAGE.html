Ada Augusta Byron

1815-1852

"More I study, the more insatiable do I feel my genius for it to be."

Here is the timeline of Ada Augusta Byron:

1815 - Ada Lovelace was born on December 10, 1815. Her birth name was Augusta Ada Byron.

1828 - When Ada was only twelve she made a small flying machine.

1833 - While at a party in England Ada first meant Charles Babbage. She was fascinated by Babbage's ideas for a machine that could calculate numbers and presumably write letters. Ada was 17 when she meant Charles Babbage machine.

1835 - Ada married William King.

1837 - Ada and Babbage tested the first computer program. And Ada wrote the first computer program.

1838 - William king and Ada Lovelace became Earl and Countess of Lovelace.

1840 - Ada had a bad habit of gambling every once in a while.

1842 - She translated an Italian mathematician's memoir in french in the subject of the Analytical Engine.

1843 - Ada published her work and used the initials A.A.L standing for Augusta Ada Lovelace. Ada wrote a book about c

1852 - Ada Lovelace got a reward for being the first computer programmer. On November 27, 1852, Lovelace died at the age of thirty-six from uterine cancer and bloodletting by her physicians. She was buried, at her request, next to her father at the Church of St. Mary Magdalene in Hucknall, Nottingham
